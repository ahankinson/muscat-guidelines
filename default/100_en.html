This part records the authorship of the main person involved as the author, in the broadest sense, of the source.

Composer/Author name (100 $a)

Enter the name of the person responsible for the work. This may include the composer (for music manuscripts and printed music), librettist (for librettos), or author of a work of music theory (for treatises). This field is linked to the authority file People. Names not yet in the authority file may be added.  

Attribution (100 $j)

Select an indicator to describe the level of certainty with the attribution:

  • Verified means the composer's full name appears on the source. This happens rarely.
  • Ascertained means the name is partially present or entirely absent from the source, but attribution is (reasonably) certain and based on comparison with concordant sources, secondary literature, or other scholarly resources. This happens in most cases.
  • Conjectural is a guess based on secondary evidence (such as secondary literature). This includes doubtful attributions.
  • Alleged means the name is present on the source, but the attribution is questionable or doubtful.
  • Misattributed means the name is on the source but is incorrect.
