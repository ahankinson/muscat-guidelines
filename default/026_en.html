Fingerprint identifier (026)

A fingerprint identifier is used to assist in the identification of antiquarian books by recording information comprising groups of characters taken from specified positions on specified pages of the book.

Guidelines established by the Institut de Recherche et dâ€™Histoire des Texte and the National Library of Scotland can be consulted online: English, French, and Italian. A translation in German is also available.

Elements of a fingerprint can be separated into different fields or can be entered as a whole in a single field.


First/second group (026 $a)

Enter the characters that belong in the first and second group.


Third/fourth group (026 $b)

Enter the characters that belong in the third and fourth group.

Date (026 $c)

Enter the date when expressed as part of a fingerprint identifier.


Number of volume/part (026 $d)

Enter the number of the volume or part when expressed as part of a fingerprint identifier.


Unparsed fingerprint (026 $e)

Enter the complete fingerprint identifier.
